                27.  Driver Checklist - Please Note:  These guidelines are provided as a basic outline of the typical steps to follow.  There are many other problems or unexpected things that could come up – always be on the lookout.  Remember to use your own common sense and if in doubt, always call your immediate supervisor with any questions.  
                <br/>Remember: Safety is our #1 priority! 
                <br/> <b>A) Dumpster Pickup:</b>
                     <br/>1) Check area for any hazards such as low cables, branches, fences, etc.   
                     <br/>2) Ensure the dumpster door is closed and secured.  Ensure there is nothing hanging over the sides of the dumpster and that it is not overloaded.
                     <br/>3) Upload a Dumpster Content Photo
                     <br/>4) Turn your PTO on and pick up the dumpster.
                     <br/>5) Cable pull truck: strap the rear of the dumpster down or check the wings are under the brackets.
                     <br/>6) Tarp the dumpster.          
                     <br/>7) Turn your PTO off, check for hazards and drive away. 
                <br/><b>B) Emptying the Dumpster:
                <br/><span class="space"></span><span class="space"></span><span class="space"></span>Any dumpster that gets emptied, depending on what type of load it is, must have one of the following to close off the record: Load photo, receipt or ticket.</b>
                     <br/>1) Proceed to the landfill as Directed on Salesforce.
                     <br/>2) Drive onto the scales.     
                     <br/>3) Receive the tickets, write your trip HBD number on it, sign the other one and return. At some landfills you will need to, weigh in, and weigh out for your receipt.       
                     <br/>4) Drive around to the current dumping site, turn your PTO on, remove your tarp, then remove your chain, then open the dumpster door and secure it open, and empty the load.             
                     <br/>5) Turn your PTO off, check for hazards and drive away. 
                     <br/>6) Close the dumpster door, secure the chain, and lock it.
                     <br/>7) Any contents not allowed at the landfills, that get put back in the dumpster – upload a clear photo to the Prohibited Photo section on salesforce.
                     <br/>8) Take a photo of your landfill receipt and upload it to the trip you are on.
                <br/><b> C) Drop Off Deliveries:</b>
                     <br/>1) When you are on the way to deliver a dumpster, call the customer to provide ETA and to confirm drop off location.  (Customer phone number and drop off location are on the record.)  
                     <br/>2) Check area for any hazards such as low cables, branches, fences, etc.  Do not drive on any grass – this is because it is easy to sink and get stuck either now at drop off, or later when we pick up the dumpster as it is heavier. 
                     <br/>3) If you have a cable pull truck, remove the rear straps. 
                     <br/>4) Turn your PTO on and drop the dumpster in the designated area.            
                     <br/>5) Turn your PTO off.          
                     <br/>6) If you have a cable pull truck, secure the straps onto the cable and tighten             
                     <br/>7) Take a photo of the dumpster ID and a photo of the dumpster’s surrounding area and upload to the record at the exact location you drop the dumpster. This helps locate the dumpster upon pickup.
